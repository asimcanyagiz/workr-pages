Workr — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 May 2026

Short version

Workr stores your tasks and projects locally on your device and (if you sign into iCloud) in your own private iCloud database. We do not collect, store, sell, or share any personal data on any server we control.

What we collect

Nothing. Workr does not have a backend. The app does not send analytics, telemetry, or crash logs to us. There is no account to create and no login flow.

What stays on your device

  • The tasks, projects, priorities, and completion timestamps you create.
  • Standard iOS / macOS preferences set by SwiftUI / SwiftData.

What goes to iCloud (only if you're signed in)

When you are signed into iCloud on the device, Apple's CloudKit framework automatically syncs your Workr data across your Apple devices using your private iCloud database. We — the developers of Workr — do not have access to this data. Only you and Apple's infrastructure can read it. This sync uses Apple's standard encryption and authentication.

If you sign out of iCloud or disable iCloud Drive for Workr, sync stops and your data stays only on the local device.

Third parties

None. Workr has no third-party SDKs, no advertising, no tracking, no analytics, and no third-party services of any kind.

Children's privacy

Workr is rated 4+ and is safe for all ages. It does not collect any data from any user, regardless of age.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy in a meaningful way, the change will be reflected in the "Last updated" date above and (for material changes) noted in the next App Store release notes.
